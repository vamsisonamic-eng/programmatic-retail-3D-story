Phase 03 · The engine room

A stock market
for attention.

How does it scale? Programmatic AI. Think of it as a lightning-fast stock market. Every single time a shopper opens an app, the store's computer asks brands: "Who wants to show an ad right now?" In a tenth of a second an automated auction runs, a winner is picked, and the ad loads. Humans simply aren't fast enough to do this — automation is the rocket fuel.

~100msper auction — brands' computers (DSP) buy, the store's system (SSP) sells, verified live
millionsof placements traded every minute

The slot opens. A shopper's screen has one empty ad space — the "stock" up for sale.

20–60ms

Bids stream in. Each brand's computer prices this exact shopper using the store's first-party data — that's the "signal fit" column.

60–80ms

Referees verify. Fake or unviewable impressions get thrown out before money moves.

~96ms

The market clears. Highest qualified bid wins the screen; everyone else is outbid. The shopper never felt a thing.

Epilogue · Keep these numbers

The trading floor, in three markets.

Same programmatic engine — DSPs bidding, SSPs selling, verifiers refereeing — expressed three different ways around the world.

🇺🇸 United States — the search floor

Born in Amazon's search bar. Amazon + Walmart hold ~85% of the market and take ~89% of every new dollar; Kroger, Target Roundel, and regional grocers like Meijer Media run the same playbook.

$60.3B → $71.1B2025 → 2026, +17.8% · now bigger than traditional TV (eMarketer)

🇯🇵 Japan — the in-store exchange

Lower e-commerce adoption pushed the market into physical stores: FamilyMart's signage network covers ~10,000 stores, synced to the 26M-download FamiPay app at the moment of decision.

¥470B → ¥900B+2024 → 2027 projection (MGSSI / CARTA)

🇮🇳 India — the velocity market

The app is the shelf. Sponsored tiles inside Blinkit, Zepto & Instamart bankroll 10-minute delivery — ads are ~15% of Blinkit's revenue; Zepto's ad revenue grew +151% in FY26.

₹3,000 → ₹4,900 cr2025 → 2026E (Storyboard18)
