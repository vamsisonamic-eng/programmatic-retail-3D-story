01 · What is retail media?

The store became the medium.

A Retail Media Network (RMN) is a retailer — Amazon, Walmart, FamilyMart, Blinkit — selling ad space on its own turf: search results, apps, in-store screens. Powered by the one thing only retailers have: what you actually buy.

Why now? Privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, Japan's APPI) are killing the third-party cookie. Retailers' first-party purchase data is the legal, precise replacement.

The engine room · Why programmatic matters

Every ad you saw was sold in ~100 milliseconds.

Retail media runs on programmatic advertising — an automated, always-on auction. Each time a shopper opens an app or passes a screen, brands' bidding computers (DSPs, the buying desks) compete for the slot, the retailer's selling computer (SSP, the sales desk) picks a winner, and independent verification partners confirm the ad was real, viewable, and brand-safe — all before the page loads. Without this plumbing, retail media couldn't exist at scale.

⚡ Scale no human can sell

Millions of ad slots per minute across apps, sites, and in-store screens. Programmatic is the only market mechanism fast enough — it's why RMNs can monetize every impression, not just premium sponsorships.

~100msper real-time auction, before the page loads

🎯 First-party data, acting live

Programmatic pipes are what let a retailer's loyalty data pick which shopper sees which ad in real time — cookie-free — and let AI pre-allocate budgets on live signals instead of last month's report.

Reference · The three models

🇺🇸

United States — the behemoth model

Born in Amazon's search bar, scaled online. Amazon + Walmart hold ~85% and capture ~89% of every new dollar — but Kroger, Target (Roundel), and regional grocers like Meijer (Meijer Media) run the same playbook. Walmart's ads: $4.4B (2024) → $6.4B (2025).

$60.3B → $71.1B2025 → 2026, +17.8% (eMarketer)
🇯🇵

Japan — the in-store screen model

Lower e-commerce adoption pushed retail media into physical stores: digital signage, shelf labels, register screens. FamilyMartVision covers ~60% of its network, synced to the FamiPay app.

¥470B → ¥900B+2024 → 2027 projection (MGSSI / CARTA)
🇮🇳

India — the quick-commerce wave

The app is the shelf. Sponsored tiles inside Blinkit, Zepto & Instamart fund 10-minute delivery — ads are already ~15% of Blinkit's revenue; Zepto's ad revenue grew +151% in FY26.
